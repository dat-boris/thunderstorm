Wen Tak Wong (Chou Pu Yuan and Co-director)

His other acting experiences in Oxford include acting as Pak Keris in Mahsuri (Malaysian Night 2000) and as an Indian Trader in Parameswara (Malaysian Night 2001). He is honoured to be given the chance to participate in Liang Zhu's Drama and Performing Arts Society's first play since its founding.

Thomas Williams (Chou Ping)

St. Hugh's College,  Archaeology and Anthropology

Commencing at the age of 6 as the letter U in the 'vowels song', my acting career progressed dizzyingly. In a series of Nativity plays, through dint of accumulating talent and experience I was gradually promoted from shepherd to Joseph. However, my main speech was somewhat ruined by me not speaking, as my mouth was full of sausage at the time.
Having recovered from the psychological scars of this harrowing experience, I went on to star as a teacher traumatized by the fact that one of his students was a donkey, and as the scarecrow who came to miraculously to life after the children sang the birdy song whilst circling around him.
But when I was told, after my performance as a giant, that I was neither a)large, nor b)loud, nor c) scary, I began to have grave doubts about my future as a serious actor, and have thus not performed since the age of 11.

Koala Ng (Chou Chung)

University College, 2nd year Mathematic & Computer Science

His first appeared in An Old Restaurant in the Urban Council Drama Festival in 1996, and followed this up with appearances in Tales of the Walled City(Hong Kong Repertory Theatre, 1997), in the Inter-Hall Drama Competition at the University of Hong Kong(2000). He has also directed Toys (1998) at the HK Secondary Schools Drama Festival and The Anthem of Autumn (1999).

Carly Vandenberg (Fan Yi)

St. John’s College, 1st year English student

Previous productions include The Tempest (Miranda) and the Trial (Collins) at the North Hampshire Theatre Festival as well as pantomime.  She is pleased to be making her Oxford debut with Thunderstorm, which offers the chance to explode the sweet and innocent reputation that has plagued her for so long.

Siran Lu (Si-Feng and Producer)

St. John’s College, 2nd year Chemist

Popularly known as “Rikko”; her interest in drama arises from co-writing a script with her friends when she is at  A level college. She has been producer of two plays and this is her acting debut in Oxford. In spare time she has had a lot of fun with the organic ring chemistry (thanks to the tutors). About Thunderstorm she said:  I am so honored to work with this enthusiastic team and I hope you will found Ssu Feng lovable, though I am quite dissimilar to her off stage. I would also like to dedicate this work to my beloved parents and friends, without whose support I can never come to it.

Louise Clarke (Lu Ma and Treasurer)

St. John’s College, 2nd Year Chemist

This is my first acting role apart from a MENCAP production, where I played Cinderella, but we only performed to our parents.  As well as playing Lu Ma, I am the treasurer. I am enjoying this as things move very quickly (p.s. need a T-shirt, come to me).  I was really, really impressed by the "Butterfly lovers" so just wanted to work with some of the people that made that work so well.  I am also really interested in east Asian culture.  I have learnt a lot about Chinese society, like the way woman are expected to be strong and hold back emotions.

Joe Zhaoyuan Ma (Lu Kuei)

Wolfson College, 1st year D. Phil  student in Physics

Einstein said that the most incomprehensible thing about the universe is that it is comprehensible, for Zhao, the most impossible thing now possible for him during last months, is that he became an part-time actor instead of a full time physicist. Although he enjoys the fun of quantum computers and fractal images, he decided to play the most illiterate and philistine but, very, very, important role in this drama--the father, the husband and the most loyal servant -- Lu Kuei.
”If I could dice for which universe I live, I hope I could choose the one we love.”

Boris Lau (Lu Ta Hai and Publicity Officer)

St. John’s College, 2nd year Computer Scientist

A brand new experience for him in terms of both Acting and Production.  His character is responsible for most of the violence and destruction in the play.  Off the stage, he makes slightly more contribution with various posters and postcards that people often find lying around Oxford.
