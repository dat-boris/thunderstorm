Introduction


Thunderstorm is an outstanding example of the modern Chinese play. Published when the author was only twenty, this play took the literary world by storm. Tsao Yu's four classic works, <Thunderstorm>, <Sunrise>, <The wildness >, <Bei Jing People>, have been performed on thousands of stages and under a multiplicity of guises, bringing enthusiasm and appreciation for the Chinese play up to an all-time high.

The story of 'Thunderstorm' unravels within a single day, but past events soon catch up with the eight characters in the large Chinese capitalist family. The story steadily reveals paradoxes arising from conflicting moral values and class differences, with certain characters seeking to rebel against the conventions of society. Passion and vigour are combined with an initially invisible yet increasingly perceptible feeling of a looming tragedy, culminating in a colossal thunderstorm. A tale which has shaken every audience, its impact reverberating in their
minds and hearts.
