Vision from Director

 

The Thunderstorm is a play of tensions, and, in the twenty-four hours in which the action takes place, we watch these tensions mount and tauten as the tragic climax swiftly approached. The setting is the home of an industrialist in north China, where a family has come together with the inexorable fatality of a Greek drama.

Here, in miniature, we witness the disintegration of the fabric of the old society and the birth pangs of the new. The head of the house, a self-made man whose preoccupation with success has made him in sensible to the ties of blood and affection, still bears within him the stifling weight of the old feudal society. The younger generation is in rebellion against the decaying order, which the older generation stand for, and the intensity of their reaction reveals new facets in the dramatic situation.

The play is an accomplished example of the Western form of drama in China. The emotional conflicts and stresses should be handled with such finesse and skillfully interwoven and counterpoised in such a way that suspense is sustained to the final curtain.

With its touch of the most sensitive social issues, it showed the greatest social concern for the plight of the workers under capitalist exploitation and an individual effort to assert personal freedom and happiness under the weight of patriarchal society. It is so ruthless in its attacks upon traditional Chinese morals and measures of family value.

Greatest artistic flaw tragedy must be more than an abject expression of despair or admission of failure.

This play will be a fusion of cultures, of arts, of people and of tragedy and exhilaration. This is an ambitious attempt to perform a Chinese Modern Drama as much authentic flavor as possible, with a cast consisting of both western and eastern origins. It will be a play of elaborate costumes with minimal settings and props. With a team of experienced stage makers, this play will indeed be a spectacular exhibition in a mix of eastern and western setting.
